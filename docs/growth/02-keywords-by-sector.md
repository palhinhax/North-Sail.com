## 8. Per-locale priority (don't translate everything day one)

Build order by market reality:

1. **PT (default market):** all sectors + web-apps axis + intent pages. This is your beachhead.
2. **EN (global authority):** web-apps axis first (international demand for "custom web app for law firm", "client portal development"), then restaurants/hotels (tourism). EN is where you build domain authority for international answer-engine citations.
3. **ES:** restaurants, hairdressers, hotels, clinics (large SMB markets), then web-apps.
4. **FR / DE:** hotels, restaurants, web-apps-for-business + law firms (high-value, less price-sensitive). DE especially: lead with privacy/security messaging.

**Adapt, don't translate.** Examples: DE buyers respond to "Datenschutz / DSGVO-konform" and security; FR to "sur-mesure" craftsmanship; ES to price + "sin comisiones"; EN-international to "no per-cover commission" and "senior engineering". Keep the slug localized (already in `routes.ts`) and the H1/value-prop market-tuned, not word-for-word.
