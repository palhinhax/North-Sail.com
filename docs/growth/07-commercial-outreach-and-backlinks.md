# 30-Day Client Acquisition Playbook + Backlink Plan

Goal: real clients in 30 days while SEO/GEO compounds. Outreach is the fast channel; content/links are the slow compounding one. Run both.

> Compliance: B2B cold outreach to businesses is generally acceptable under GDPR (legitimate interest) when relevant and with an opt-out, but **PT/EU marketing rules apply** — always include who you are and an easy opt-out, don't scrape personal emails, prefer public business addresses, and honor removal instantly.

---

## 1. Who to contact (target lists)

| Segment                      | Where to find them                        | Signal to prioritise                                |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Restaurants / cafés          | Google Maps, TheFork, Zomato, Instagram   | No website, or "link in bio" only, or slow/old site |
| Hairdressers / barbers       | Instagram, Google Maps                    | Booking via DMs only                                |
| Clinics (dental, physio)     | Google Maps, professional directories     | No online booking                                   |
| Gyms / studios               | Instagram, Google Maps                    | Schedule only on stories                            |
| Hotels / local accommodation | Booking.com, Airbnb, Google               | Only on platforms, no direct site                   |
| **Law firms**                | Bar association lists, LinkedIn, Google   | Outdated site, no client portal                     |
| **Consultancies / agencies** | LinkedIn, Clutch                          | Manual delivery, email-heavy                        |
| **Real estate**              | Idealista, Google                         | Listings only on portals                            |
| Businesses with old sites    | BuiltWith (old WordPress), PageSpeed < 50 | Slow, not mobile-friendly                           |

Build a sheet: name, segment, contact, website status, the one specific problem you spotted. Personalisation = reply rate.

---

## 2. The irresistible offer (entry wedge)

Pick one and lead with it:

- **Local/SMB:** "1 month free, no card. We'll build your site (and your reservations/bookings) and you only pay if you want to keep it." (Matches your canonical trial — strongest hook.)
- **Old-site owners:** "Free 5-minute video audit of your current site + a redesign you can try free for a month."
- **Companies / web-apps:** not free-build — instead **"Free 30-minute scoping call + a fixed-price proposal for a focused first version."** (Premium buyers distrust "free build".)

## 4. LinkedIn (companies / web-apps axis)

- **Connection note (law firm / consultancy):** "Hi [Name] — I build secure client portals and internal tools for [firms/consultancies]. Not selling here, just connecting with people modernising how they work."
- **Day +2 message:** "Thanks for connecting. Quick one: how does [Firm] currently share documents and status with clients — email, a drive, or a portal? I build portals that replace the email back-and-forth; happy to share an idea specific to your intake if useful."
- **Value post cadence (2/week):** repurpose blog cornerstones (#9 website vs web app, #14 law-firm portals, #28 cheap vs custom). End with a soft CTA.

## 5. Objection handling

| Objection                                 | Response                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| ----------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Too expensive"                           | "Entry is €15/month including domain, hosting and maintenance — less than the hours you'd spend on a DIY builder. Custom apps we scope to a focused first version so the initial cost stays controlled." |
| "I already have a website"                | "Great — is it fast on mobile and bringing in bookings? I'll send a free 5-min audit; if it's solid, I'll tell you so."                                                                                  |
| "I use Instagram / a platform"            | "Those are rented audiences that charge commission and own your data. Your own site + bookings keeps both — alongside them, not instead."                                                                |
| "I don't have time"                       | "That's the point — we build and maintain it. You approve, we do the rest."                                                                                                                              |
| "Can I trust the data is safe?" (company) | "Data is isolated per client, SSL-encrypted, role-based access. For law firms, confidentiality is the default design."                                                                                   |
| "What if I want to leave?"                | "Monthly, cancel anytime. We document a clean handover; you have full right of use while subscribed."                                                                                                    |
| "Why subscription not one-off?"           | "Software needs hosting, security updates and changes. The subscription keeps it alive and improving instead of rotting after launch."                                                                   |

## 6. Five-message follow-up sequence (per prospect)

1. **Day 0 — Email 1:** the pitch (section 3).
2. **Day 3 — Email 2 (value):** "Here's a 90-second mockup/video of what I'd do for [Business]." (A real screenshot or Loom — by far the highest-converting touch.)
3. **Day 7 — Email 3 (proof):** short case/illustrative example in their sector + "want one like this?"
4. **Day 12 — Email 4 (objection pre-empt):** "Most [restaurants] worry about X — here's how it works… 1 month free, no card."
5. **Day 18 — Email 5 (break-up):** "I'll stop here so I'm not a bother — if reservations on your own site ever become a priority, reply and I'll set it up. All the best." (Break-up emails get strong reply rates.)

Stop on any reply; move to a call. Keep a CRM (even a spreadsheet) with stage + next action.

## 7. Phone / call script (15 min)

1. **Open (15s):** "Hi [Name], [Your name] from NorthSail — I build [websites with reservations / client portals]. Caught you at an OK time for 5 minutes?"
2. **Diagnose (2–3 min):** "How do customers book / how do you share docs today? What's annoying about it?" — _listen, note their words._
3. **Reframe (1 min):** mirror their pain → name the outcome ("so bookings wouldn't clog your phone anymore").
4. **Offer:** local → "1 month free, no card, I build it first." company → "I'll scope a fixed-price first version."
5. **Close to a next step (not a sale):** "I'll send a quick mockup by [day] — if you like it, we go live; if not, no cost. Deal?"

## 8. Campaign landing page (for paid/outreach traffic)

Build a focused `/lp/[campaign]` (noindex) per campaign so ads/outreach don't dilute the SEO pages:

- One headline matched to the campaign ("Restaurant reservations on your own site — 1 month free").
- 3 bullets (no commission · digital menu + WhatsApp · we build it for you).
- One form (reuse `LeadForm`) + one CTA. No nav distractions.
- Social proof + the trial guarantee. Mark `robots: noindex` (the metadata builder supports `noindex`).

## 9. Backlink & authority plan (compounds SEO + GEO)

Priority order (effort-adjusted):

1. **Agency directories (do first — also entity disambiguation):** Clutch, Sortlist, The Manifest, GoodFirms, DesignRush, Google Business Profile (category _Website designer / Software company_). Each links + categorises you as a web/software company. Add all to `organizationSchema().sameAs`.
2. **Local PT business directories:** PAI, Hardmob-equivalents, local chambers of commerce, startup hubs (e.g., regional incubators). Relevance + local signal.
3. **Niche placements:** guest posts / partnerships with restaurant-supplier blogs, salon-software roundups, legal-tech newsletters. 4–6 quality posts > 50 junk links.
4. **Free tools / linkable assets:** a "website cost calculator" or "is your site mobile-friendly?" checker on-site → naturally earns links and shares; also great AEO bait.
5. **Reviews:** ask every happy client for a Google + Clutch review naming what you built ("restaurant booking site", "client portal"). Reviews drive both trust and entity/use-case association for LLMs.
6. **Partnerships / referrals:** accountants, POS resellers, real-estate networks, bar-association vendor lists — co-marketing and referral links.
7. **Digital PR:** a small data study ("we analysed 100 PT restaurant websites — X% have no online booking") → pitch to local tech/business press for editorial links.

**Avoid:** paid link farms, irrelevant directories, exact-match anchor spam. Keep anchors natural and category-consistent ("NorthSail", "web design company NorthSail", "custom web app developer").

## 10. 30-day outreach rhythm

- **Daily:** 20 cold emails (local) + 10 LinkedIn touches (company) + reply to everything within 2h.
- **Weekly:** 5 mockup/video touches (Email 2) for warmest prospects · 2 blog posts · 2 directory submissions.
- **Targets (realistic):** 400 emails → ~8–12% open-to-reply on personalised B2B → 30–50 replies → 8–15 calls → 3–6 paying clients in month 1. Mockup touches roughly double conversion.
